Carbonara Recipe

Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that is beloved for its creamy texture and rich flavor. Made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper, it is a simple yet indulgent meal that has been enjoyed for generations. The origins of carbonara are somewhat debated, but it is widely believed to have been created in the mid-20th century in Rome. Some say it was invented by Italian charcoal workers, while others believe it was a result of American soldiers bringing bacon and eggs to Italy during World War II. Regardless of its origins, carbonara remains a favorite comfort food for many, thanks to its delicious combination of savory ingredients and its satisfying, hearty nature.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup Pecorino romano cheese
  • 1 cup pancietta
  • 1 tbs black pepper
  • 1 tbs olive oil
  • 1 tbs salt
  • 1/4 cup parsley
  • 1/4 cup basil
  • 1/4 cup garlic
  • 1/4 cup onion
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup white win
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Steps to making Carbonara

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente, about 8-10 minutes. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water and drain the rest.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and Pecorino Romano cheese until well combined.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Remove the skillet from heat and add the cooked spaghetti to the pancetta and garlic mixture. Toss to combine.
  5. Slowly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly to create a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add some reserved pasta water, a little at a time, until desired consistency is reached.
  6. Season with black pepper and salt to taste. Add parsley and basil, and toss to combine.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with additional grated cheese and a squeeze of lemon juice if desired.
